  • Carbon Emissions

    • Power Generation
      • Electricity generation is responsible for about 42.5% global CO2 emissions. Of the 42.5% about 73% can be attributed to coal fired power plants, which emit 950g of CO2 every kilowatt-hour. For plants that run on renewable energy like hydro, wind, solar PV, and solar thermal output farless CO2, which can be attributed to their construction. For example a solar PV system emits between 60g to 150g of CO2 every kilowatt-hour, a wind turbine 3g - 22g and a hydropower plant 4g. A stark contrast to the 950g every kilowatt-hour.
    • Agriculture, Livestock, and Farming
      • In 2018 greenhouse gas emissions related to agriculture totaled 698 million metric tons which was down 2 percent in the last decade: 12.3% CO2, 36.2% CH4, 51.4% N2O . This represented 10.5% of all emissions when measured by the economic sector. Carbon sinks include forest management to increase carbon in forests, increases in tree carbon stocks in settlements, conversion of agricultural to forest land, and crop management practices that increase carbon in agricultural soils.
    • Transportation
      • Transportation is one of the largest contributors to U.S. greenhouse gas emissions. According to the inventory of the U.S. Greenhouse Gas Emissions and sinks 1990-2018, transportation accounted for the largest portion (28%) of total U.S. GHG. According to Hannah Ritchie of Our World in Data, transportation demands are only expected to grow in the coming years, with population increases and the rise of incomes more and more people will be accessing cars, trains and flights. In Energy Technology perspectives report by the IEA, the IEA expects global transport to double and car ownership to increase by 60%.
